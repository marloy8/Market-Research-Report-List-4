<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) and Real-time PCR (qPCR) are advanced techniques used in molecular biology for precise quantification of DNA and RNA. Digital PCR involves partitioning target DNA into thousands of individual reactions, providing absolute quantification of the target. Real-time PCR, on the other hand, offers continuous monitoring of the amplification of the DNA target in real-time.</p><p>The Digital PCR and Real-time PCR market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing adoption of these techniques in research, clinical diagnostics, and forensics. The market is driven by the growing prevalence of infectious diseases, genetic disorders, and cancer, which require accurate and sensitive methods for detection. In addition, technological advancements in dPCR and qPCR platforms, automation of workflows, and expanding applications in personalized medicine are fueling the market growth.</p><p>Key trends in the Digital PCR and Real-time PCR market include the development of multiplex assays, integration of artificial intelligence for data analysis, and increasing demand for point-of-care testing solutions.

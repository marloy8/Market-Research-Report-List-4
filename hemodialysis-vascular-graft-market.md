<p><p>Hemodialysis vascular grafts are classified into different types based on the materials used, such as polyester, polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), polyurethane, and biological materials. Polyester grafts are cost-effective and durable, while PTFE grafts have high patency rates and flexibility. Polyurethane grafts are biocompatible and resistant to kinking. Biological materials, such as bovine or porcine tissues, offer biocompatibility and promote natural tissue growth. Each type of vascular graft has unique characteristics that cater to different patient needs in hemodialysis treatments.</p></p>

<p><p>Dental materials for restoration can be categorized into three main types: Ceramic, Amalgam, and Composite. Ceramic materials are often preferred for their natural tooth-like appearance and durability. Amalgam materials, commonly known as silver fillings, are cost-effective and strong, but they are less aesthetically pleasing. Composite materials are versatile and can be color-matched to the patient's natural teeth, making them a popular choice for visible restorations. Each type of material has its own advantages and considerations when choosing the best option for a dental restoration.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Round Bodied Needle</li><li>Blunt Point Needle</li><li>Reverse Cutting Needle</li><li>Conventional Cutting Needle</li><li>Spatula Needle</li><li>Tapercut Needle</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Suture needles are available in various types to meet the specific needs of different surgical procedures. Round bodied needles are ideal for soft tissue, while blunt point needles are used for delicate tissues to prevent damage. Reverse cutting needles have a cutting edge on the backside for increased tensile strength. Conventional cutting needles are sharp on the inside curve for easy penetration. Spatula needles are flat and used for ophthalmic surgeries. Tapercut needles have a tapering cutting edge for precise suturing in tough tissues.</p></p>

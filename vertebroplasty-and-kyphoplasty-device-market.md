<p><p>Vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices are minimally invasive procedures used to treat vertebral compression fractures (VCFs) caused by osteoporosis, tumors, or trauma. Vertebroplasty involves the injection of bone cement into the fractured vertebra to stabilize it, while kyphoplasty involves the insertion of a balloon device to create a cavity before injecting the bone cement. These procedures help relieve pain, restore vertebral height, and improve the patient's quality of life.</p><p>The Vertebroplasty and Kyphoplasty Device Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.3% during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as the rising incidence of osteoporosis and VCFs, increasing elderly population, advancements in technology leading to the development of new and innovative products, and growing awareness among patients and healthcare professionals about the benefits of these procedures. Additionally, the minimally invasive nature of vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices, shorter recovery times, and lower risk of complications compared to traditional open surgery are driving the market growth.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing demand for vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices in emerging economies, increasing adoption of minimally invasive procedures, and a shift towards outpatient settings for these treatments. Manufacturers are also focusing on developing advanced technologies and materials to enhance the efficacy and safety of these devices, further driving market growth.</p></p>

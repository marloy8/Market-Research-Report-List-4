<p><p>Canine arthritis treatment market includes Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs) and opioids. NSAIDs are commonly prescribed for dogs with arthritis to reduce pain and inflammation. They work by blocking the production of certain enzymes that cause inflammation. Opioids are strong pain relievers that are sometimes used for severe cases of arthritis in dogs. They work by binding to receptors in the brain and spinal cord to decrease the perception of pain. Both types of medications are commonly used in veterinary medicine to manage canine arthritis.</p></p>

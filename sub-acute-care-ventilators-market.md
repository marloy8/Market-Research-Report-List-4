<p><p>Sub-acute care ventilators are classified into two main types: invasive and noninvasive. Invasive sub-acute care ventilators require the insertion of a tube into the patient's airway to assist with breathing. Noninvasive sub-acute care ventilators deliver air pressure through a mask or nasal prongs without the need for intubation. Both types of ventilators are used in sub-acute care settings to provide respiratory support for patients with compromised lung function or respiratory failure.</p></p>

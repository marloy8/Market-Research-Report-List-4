<p><p>Radioimmunotherapy is a medical treatment that uses a combination of tumor antigens, radionuclides, and antibodies to specifically target and destroy cancer cells. Tumor antigens are proteins found on the surface of cancer cells, radionuclides are radioactive atoms that emit energy to kill the targeted cells, and antibodies are proteins that bind to the tumor antigens, delivering the radionuclides directly to the cancer cells. This targeted approach minimizes damage to healthy tissue and shows promise in treating various types of cancer.</p></p>

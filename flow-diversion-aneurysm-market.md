<p><p>Flow diversion aneurysm is a minimally invasive endovascular procedure used to treat complex intracranial aneurysms by diverting blood flow away from the aneurysm. This technique involves placing a stent-like device (flow diverter) across the neck of the aneurysm, causing blood to flow through the device and away from the aneurysm, ultimately leading to its shrinkage and eventual healing.</p><p>The Flow Diversion Aneurysm Market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing prevalence of intracranial aneurysms, advancements in endovascular technologies, and the growing demand for minimally invasive treatment options. The market is also driven by the rising adoption of flow diversion procedures by neurosurgeons and interventional radiologists, as these techniques offer better outcomes and shorter recovery times for patients.</p><p>Key trends in the Flow Diversion Aneurysm Market include the development of next-generation flow diverters with improved safety and efficacy profiles, increasing investment in research and development activities, and expanding applications of flow diversion techniques beyond intracranial aneurysms. <p><p>Flow diversion aneurysm market is categorized into Silk Flow Diverter, Pipeline Embolization Device, Surpass Flow Diverter, and Others. Silk Flow Diverter is a metal mesh tube that redirects blood flow away from the aneurysm. Pipeline Embolization Device is a braided cylindrical stent that diverts blood flow away from the aneurysm. Surpass Flow Diverter is a flexible mesh tube that serves the same purpose. Other flow diversion devices in the market include p64 Flow Modulation Device and FRED Flow Diverter.</p></p>

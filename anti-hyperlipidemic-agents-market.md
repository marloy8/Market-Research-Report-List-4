<p><p>The anti-hyperlipidemic agents market comprises various types of drugs including statins, fibrates, niacin, bile acid sequestrants, and cholesterol absorption inhibitors. Statins are the most commonly prescribed and work by blocking an enzyme needed to make cholesterol. Fibrates help lower triglycerides and raise HDL cholesterol levels. Niacin also helps raise HDL cholesterol while lowering LDL cholesterol. Bile acid sequestrants bind to bile acids in the intestines, preventing cholesterol absorption. Cholesterol absorption inhibitors lower LDL cholesterol by blocking its absorption in the intestines.</p></p>

<p><p>Sterilization monitoring involves ensuring that the sterilization process effectively eliminates all microorganisms from medical equipment. Biological monitoring uses living organisms to assess sterility, such as spores. Chemical monitoring involves using chemicals that change color when exposed to certain sterilization conditions. Mechanical monitoring includes indicators like time, temperature, and pressure gauges. Each type of monitoring plays a crucial role in confirming the effectiveness of the sterilization process and maintaining the safety of medical equipment for patient use.</p></p>

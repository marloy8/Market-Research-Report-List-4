<p><p>Polylactic acid, or PLA, is a biodegradable polymer with three main types: Poly(L-lactic)acid, Poly(D-lactic)acid, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid. Poly(L-lactic)acid is derived from L-lactic acid, Poly(D-lactic)acid from D-lactic acid, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid from a mix of both L- and D-lactic acid. Each type of PLA has its own unique properties and applications. Poly(L-lactic)acid is known for its high heat resistance, Poly(D-lactic)acid for its flexibility, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid for being a cost-effective option.</p></p>

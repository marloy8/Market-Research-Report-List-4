<p><p>Sponge rubber materials come in various forms, with closed-cell rubber being one of the most common types. Closed-cell rubber is dense and provides excellent insulation and cushioning properties. On the other hand, open-cell rubber has interconnected air pockets, making it softer and more flexible. Other markets for sponge rubber materials include neoprene and EPDM, which offer unique characteristics such as oil resistance and weatherproofing. Each type has its own advantages and is used in a wide range of applications across industries.</p></p>

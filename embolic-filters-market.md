<p><ul><li>Upper Chamber Embolic Filter</li><li>Inferior Chamber Embolic Filter</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Upper chamber embolic filters are small devices placed in the upper chamber of the heart to prevent blood clots from traveling into the bloodstream and causing a blockage. These filters are typically used in patients at risk for stroke or pulmonary embolism. Inferior chamber embolic filters are similar devices placed in the inferior chamber of the heart to protect against blood clots entering the circulatory system and causing complications. Both types of filters are used in clinical settings to reduce the risk of embolic events and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
